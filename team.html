Meet our Team

Chef Masato Kuroda – Master of Kaiseki Art

Chef Masato Kuroda is an experienced professional, committed to preserving the elegance and philosophy of kaiseki ryokan. His passion for transforming seasonal ingredients into dishes that convey calmness, precision, and beauty has driven him to refine traditional techniques throughout his career. For him, cooking kaiseki is a way to honor Japanese culture and offer each guest an intimate and carefully crafted experience.

Hiroshi Tanaka – Assistant Chef

Hiroshi Tanaka brings energy, dedication, and a deep curiosity for traditional Japanese cuisine. Passionate about attention to detail and the balance of flavors, he has chosen to train in the art of kaiseki ryokan because he sees it as a way to express respect for ingredients and harmony with nature. His enthusiasm and discipline make him an essential support within the culinary team.

Kobayashi Haruto - Main Supplier

Haruto is a calm and meticulous man with over twenty years of experience working with local farmers, fishermen, and artisans. He is responsible for selecting every ingredient that enters the kaiseki restaurant, from seasonal vegetables to handcrafted ceramics used for plating.

Sakuraba Yui - Manager

Yui is a young, creative, and energetic woman who blends tradition with modernity. A graduate in digital marketing, she manages the restaurant’s social media presence, promotional campaigns, and collaborations with culinary influencers.

Kobayashi Haruto - apprentice

Sora is a shy but passionate young man who has just begun his apprenticeship in the kaiseki kitchen. He is still learning the basics, from sharpening knives to preparing dashi with the proper patience.

Ajikitcho Mission

La misión de nuestro kaiseki ryōri es honrar la esencia de la estacionalidad japonesa (shun) y celebrar la belleza de la simplicidad. Cada plato nace del respeto absoluto hacia los ingredientes, seleccionados en su momento más puro, y preparados mediante técnicas transmitidas de generación en generación. Nuestro propósito es ofrecer una experiencia que armonice sabor, estética y serenidad, guiando al comensal a través de un viaje sensorial donde cada bocado expresa la relación íntima entre la naturaleza y la tradición culinaria japonesa. Buscamos crear un espacio donde el tiempo se desacelera, permitiendo que los visitantes perciban la sutileza de cada aroma, textura y color, y donde la hospitalidad (omotenashi) se exprese no solo en el servicio, sino en cada detalle del ambiente. El kaiseki no es solo comida; es un puente espiritual entre la estación, el artesano, el chef y el huésped.
